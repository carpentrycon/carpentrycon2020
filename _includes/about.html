• What are The Carpentries and CarpentryCon?

    The Carpentries is a fiscally sponsored project of Community Initiatives teaching foundational computational and data science skills to researchers.

    The theme of the CarpentryCon 2020 conference is Growing inclusive, computational communities and leaders.

    Learn more about The Carpentries, CarpentryCon and the CarpentryCon 2020 Dates, Location and Theme.

  • When and where will it be held?


    CarpentryCon 2020 will be held from June 29 - July 1st, 2020, at the University of Wisconsin - Madison, United States. The city of Madison is sandwiched between two freshwater lakes, features an iconic state capitol building in the city center, and is a short trip from several other large US cities (Chicago, Milwaukee, Minneapolis). The University of Wisconsin - Madison sits along the shores of Lake Mendota, including a student union with a lakeside terrace.

    We are very grateful for the generous support of the University of Wisconsin - Madison in hosting this event. To help make this a truly international event, we will be offering travel support to facilitate attendance from as wide a group as possible.

  • Who is organising it?

    The conference is being organised by the CarpentryCon 2020 Task Force, on behalf of The Carpentries.

  • Will I fit in?

    We are working hard to ensure that everyone feels welcome at CarpentryCon 2020. Attendees must be prepared to abide by our Code of Conduct. You will be able to give us full details of your childcare and mobility needs and dietary preferences at the time of registeration.

  • What about my special needs?

    We want to make CarpentryCon 2020 as accessible as possible. At the registration stage, there will be plenty of space for you to tell us about any special needs we need to accommodate, whether they be around childcare, mobility, dietary preferences, or anything else that you need us to take into account to make CarpentryCon 2020 work for you.
